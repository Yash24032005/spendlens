# GTM — SpendLens Go-to-Market Plan

## Exact Target User

Not "startups." The specific person is: **Engineering Manager or CTO at a Series A / seed-stage startup, 10–50 employees, $500K–$5M ARR, using 3+ AI dev tools across their engineering team.**

They're cost-conscious but not penny-pinching — they approved the AI tools in the first place. They feel mild guilt every time the Stripe bill arrives because they're not sure if the spend is justified. They've never audited it because "auditing AI tools" isn't a Google search that obviously exists. They manage a team of 4–15 engineers and are starting to feel the AI tool sprawl.

Secondary user: **Ops/Finance lead at a 20–100 person startup** who gets asked by the CEO "why is our AI tool spend $8K/month?" and has no good answer.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days (Zero Paid Budget)

**Week 1: Seed posts on communities where CTOs are**
- Post on Hacker News as "Show HN: I built a free AI spend auditor — found $400/mo in waste on my own team." Personal, specific, includes the actual finding.
- Post on r/ExperiencedDevs: "Our team was paying for Cursor Business + GitHub Copilot simultaneously. $X/mo wasted. Made a tool to check."
- Cold DM 20 CTOs/EMs on LinkedIn who've tweeted about AI tool costs in the last 60 days.

**Week 2: Leverage the audit results themselves**
- Every shared audit URL is a distribution artifact. Put "Made with SpendLens by Credex" in the page footer with a link.
- DM 5 AI newsletter writers (tldr.tech, The Batch, The Rundown AI) and offer them "free audit + interesting data on how startups are spending on AI tools."

**Week 3: Partner distribution**
- Reach out to Cursor's Discord / community channels — offer to post a tool that helps users figure out which Cursor plan is right for them. Not "anti-Cursor" — "help you right-size."
- Contact indie hacker communities (Indie Hackers, MicroConf Slack) — founders are extremely price-sensitive on tooling.

**Week 4: Data-driven content**
- By now we have ~50 audits. Write a short post: "What 50 startup AI spend audits taught us." Aggregate anonymized findings. This gets picked up by tech newsletters organically.

**Credex already has inbound relationships with companies that over-purchased AI credits.** Those companies know other startups who are paying retail. The supply side of Credex's marketplace is also the distribution channel — they can tell their networks "we excess credits, here's a tool to see if you're overpaying." Credex's existing customer base can be the first 20 promoters.

No other tool in this space has that supply-side relationship. It's the thing only Credex could do.
